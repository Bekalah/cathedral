# The Vision

## Why Cathedral Exists

I have chronic PTSD. Creative work is how I survive. But most creative tools aren't built for people like me.

They assume you can focus for hours. They punish you for stopping. They're designed for neurotypical brains in neurotypical bodies having neurotypical days.

I needed something different.

---

## The Problem

Every time I wanted to create, I had to choose:
- Music OR art OR writing OR research
- Fun OR serious
- Play OR work
- Ancient wisdom OR modern technology

These are false choices. The most interesting people I know don't think in categories.

Adam Jones doesn't separate visual art from music. Richard D. James doesn't separate sound design from humor. Hilma af Klint didn't separate spirituality from painting. Remedios Varo didn't separate the surreal from the scientific.

Why should I?

---

## The Solution

Cathedral is everything at once.

**It's a game** — You can play as 22 different arcana characters, make choices, explore a world.

**It's a music studio** — 10 synthesizers, Solfeggio frequencies, sacred geometry visualization.

**It's an art tool** — Pattern generation, rare pigment simulation, master technique recreation.

**It's a design system** — Luxury aesthetic, sacred mathematics, museum-quality output.

**It's a research library** — Real sources, real correspondences, real history.

**It's a grimoire** — A living book that grows with you.

And you can switch between any of these instantly. Working on music and suddenly want to paint? One click. Playing the game and need to ground yourself? One click. Researching medieval manuscripts and want to make a synthesizer patch inspired by what you found? One click.

## The 22 Living Arcana

The Major Arcana aren't just cards. They're playable characters.

Each one has:
- **A tradition** — Taoist Void Master, Hermetic Alchemist, Kabbalistic Walker, etc.
- **A frequency** — From 174Hz (grounding) to 963Hz (transcendence)
- **A visual style** — Color palettes, sacred geometries, art movements
- **A story arc** — The journey they take through the Circuitum99

**The Fool / Rebecca Respawn** is mine. The one who keeps dying and coming back. The one who walks off cliffs because she has to see what's down there. The one who starts at 0 and ends at infinity.

You can create your own.

## The Codex 144:99

This is the mathematical heart.

**144 nodes** — Each one a point in consciousness space, with element, frequency, correspondences.

**99 gates** — Transformation portals between nodes, based on harmonic relationships.

**The ratio** — 144:99 ≈ 1.4545... A number that appears in sacred geometry, music theory, and the proportions of Gothic cathedrals.

It's not mystical mumbo-jumbo. It's pattern recognition. The same mathematics that makes music sound good makes architecture feel sacred makes visual composition work. The Codex is a map of those patterns.

## The Living Grimoire

In medieval times, a grimoire was a personal magic book. You copied spells into it. You added your own notes. It grew with you.

The Stone Grimoire is that, but alive.

**8 Chapels** — Different traditions (Hermetic, Alchemical, Kabbalistic, etc.)

**144 Folios** — Pages that generate based on your discoveries

**Real Research** — Every "spell" is backed by actual historical sources

As you play the game, explore the Cathedral, make art, compose music—the grimoire records it. It connects what you're doing to the traditions it comes from. It grows.

And you can export it as actual pages. Print them. Bind them. Make a real book.

## Trauma-Safe Design

This isn't a feature. It's the foundation.

**No jump scares** — Everything fades, nothing sudden

**Emergency exit** — On every screen, one click away

**Grounding exercises** — Built into the interface, accessible anytime

**Progress preservation** — You never lose work because you had to stop

**Flexible pacing** — No timers, no penalties, no "you lose" for taking a break

**Sensory controls** — Sound off, motion reduced, contrast adjusted—all remembered

I designed this for myself first. For the days when I can barely function but still need to make something. For the moments when creation is the only thing keeping me together but my nervous system is on fire.

If you don't need these features, you won't notice them. If you do, they might save you.

## The Aesthetic

I hated graphic design school. All those flat boxes, cartoon gradients, pill buttons. Design by committee. Design that looks like everything else.

Cathedral looks like walking into Tiffany's if Tiffany's sold grimoires.

- **Obsidian blacks** with warm undertones
- **Burnished gold** like old jewelry
- **Champagne** like aged paper
- **No boxes** — content breathes
- **No emoji** — typography speaks
- **No cartoon** — depth and dimension

Bodoni Moda for headlines. DM Sans for body. The luxury of restraint.

If that's you, welcome home.

**Of Circuits** — We're doing it with silicon now. The goal is the same. A space where everything connects. Where the boundaries between art forms dissolve. Where you can lose yourself safely.
